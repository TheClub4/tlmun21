TOPIC:
The Postwar Reorganization of Germany and Europe

Six brutal years have passed since the onset of the Second World War. The Allies have begun their march into the German heartland. In the West, British and American forces have begun their breach into the Siegfried Line. In the East, the Soviet Union has prepared for the final blow, the Red Army setting their sights on Berlin. The end of the war was near, yet what shall be the future of Europe?


The Yalta Conference was held for this purpose. Beginning from the 4th of February, 1945, Premier Joseph V. Stalin, President Franklin D. Roosevelt and Prime Minister Winston Churchill met in the resort city of Yalta, situated in the Crimean Peninsula. As the second of three significant wartime conferences among the “Big Three”, it sought to form a lasting peace in post-war Europe, enshrined by an order of collective security with self-determination for all liberated peoples of Europe.


The future of Europe is in your hands. Shall Europe become a bastion of eternal peace and self-determination or shall Europe fall into the hollows of history as a divided continent, driven by the interests of superpowers? You shall decide.
