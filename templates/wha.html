TOPIC A:
Improving the accessibility of vaccines

TOPIC B:
Addressing the effects of bioterrorism

The World Health Assembly (WHA) is the main governing body of the World Health Organisation (WHO), comprising of 194 member states. Focus on policy-setting, the WHA seeks to continue with the planning and attainment of new health goals, while formulating strategies to achieve such objectives.


Throughout the years, the WHA has worked on various health issues and matters, such as communicable & non-communicable diseases, food security and bioterrorism. In addition, it concerns itself with the accessibility of vaccines for deadly diseases, such as Malaria, smallpox and Polio.


As the question of vaccines have come to light in these trying times, it is undoubted that such is at the minds of the representatives at the WHA. In addition, one cannot forget the concerns over bioterrorism, for awareness has overpowered ignorance.
